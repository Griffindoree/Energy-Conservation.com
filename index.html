What is Energy Conservation?

Energy conservation is the systematic effort to use less energy by using it more wisely and responsibly. Its goal is to reduce waste and mitigate the negative impacts on the environment, the economy, and society that come with energy production and use.


In practical terms, energy conservation means using energy-efficient technologies and adopting responsible energy consumption habits to save money, protect the environment, and ensure a sustainable energy supply for the future. It is a professional and responsible approach to managing our energy resources.

About our Project

This project aims to address the critical issue of energy conservation among grade 10 students at the University of Saint Louis Tuguegarao (USLT). With a primary focus on educating the students, the project aims to instill a deep understanding of the importance of energy conservation and provide practical sources to implement sustainable energy practices.

The objectives include raising energy awareness, promoting behavioral change towards energy efficiency, and equipping students with knowledge about energy-efficient technologies. It also encourages students to become advocates for energy conservation within their peer groups and families while considering the limitations to ensure the success of the project implementation.

While the primary beneficiaries are grade 10 students, the project's broader implications may extend to the university, the local community, the environment, and future generations. Ultimately, this project seeks to inspire a culture of energy conservation, foster responsible energy consumption habits, and contribute to a more sustainable and environmentally conscious society.
